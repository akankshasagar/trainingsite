Types of Scams/Frauds

Electricity Bill Scam

Many users gets messages on WhatsApp and via SMS from unknown numbers, claiming that their electricity will be cut off if the bill is not paid. But instead of redirecting you to official government portal, you're asked to contact a certain number.

WhatsApp QR code scam

The fraudster sends a text message saying they’ve won something along with a QR code. The message will prompt you to scan the code, enter a specified amount, and ‘receive’ the cash.

Impersonation Scams

Impersonation scams involve the fraudster pretending to be a victim's friend, a family member, or an acquaintance. The scammer contacts a user from an unknown number, noting that the profile picture may be familiar, then immediately asks for money in an emergency.

Malicious Links

Fraudsters send external links that lead the users directed to the browser to fill a survey promising a freebie. Victim completes the survey and enters sensitive information such as name, address, bank details, and email address.  Later on, the WhatsApp scammer use these details for attempting theft or selling on to third parties

Frequently Forwarded Messages

Users can see a small warning on their inbox that helps them know that a specific message has been forwarded many times. if the message is forwarded many times, it is mostly going to be spam or circulating fake news, videos, or something more sinister.
